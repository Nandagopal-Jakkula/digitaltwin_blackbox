Leak Detection Algorithm:
Leak detection is based on the hydraulic relationship between inflow and outflow sensors, as well as pressure gradients. The following equations govern the process:
Mass Balance Method: 
Qleak = Qin – Qout.
If the difference between inflow and outflow exceeds a preset threshold, a leak is suspected.
Pressure Drop Correlation: 
?P = Pentering – Pexiting
Pexiting = pressure of the water exiting the pipe. (Second sensor)
Pentering = pressure of the water entering the pipe. (first sensor)
If a significant pressure drop is associated with a flow imbalance, it strengthens leak confidence.
Leak Area Estimation (Orifice Equation):
A=Q/(Cd?(2?Pe/?))
Where: 
A: Leak Area (m2)
Q: Leak flow rate (m3/s)
Cd: Discharge coefficient (0.6-0.8)
?: Fluid density (kg/m3)
?Pe: Pressure difference (Pa)
Pout = earth pressure above the leak.
Pin = pressure captured by the sensor inside the pipe after the leak.
From the estimated area, the Leak diameter is calculated as: D=2?(A/?)
Where soil density comes into play (Earth pressure)
Soil density is used to calculate external pressure on the pipe:
Psoil=?soil?g?h
Where:
* ?soi? = soil density (kg/m³)
* g = 9.81 m/s²
* h = burial depth (m)

* Burial depth = 2 m
